Dooars


THE IDEL PLACE FOR TOURITS TO VISIT FOR ENJOYING THE BUETY OF NATURE

West Bengal Tourism Department

The Dooars or Duars are at the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas in North-East India Bhutan. Duar means door and region forms the gateway to Bhutan From India. There are 18 passages or Through which Bhutanise people can communicate to the people leaving in the plans. The economy Dooars is based on three T's- Tea , Tourism & Timber. The main Industry of Dooars region is the tea industry. Thousand of people are engaged in tea estates and factories. Several people are also engaged in the cultivation of betel nuts which contributes to the economy. Cultivation of other crops is done mainly for local consumption.

The area is dotted by several national Parks and wildlife sanctuaries which attracts a lot of tourists from all over India and abroad, making it an important contributor to the economy and also employer of number of people. The timber industry, flourishes in this region. A number of saw mills, plywood industries and other allied business act as an important contributor to the economy. Dooars act as a doorway to Bhutan,thus export-import industry also flourishes in the area. The towns of Jaigaon, Siliguri and Phuntsholing are important hubs of the export-import indistry.

How to reach

By Air: The nearest airport is Bagdogra connecting, Kolkata, Delhi and other Major cities


By Train: There is a raliway service connecting Cooch Behar with Kolkata, Shealdah & Howrah via New Jalpaiguri (NJP) station. One can also avail the railway service via Alipurduar. .


By Road: Siliguri is the gateway to Dooars. Cooch Behar, being the headquaters of North Bengal State Transport Corporation, is well connected by long distance bus routes to Siliguri as well as Kolkata, Jalpaiguri and other major cities of the State.
